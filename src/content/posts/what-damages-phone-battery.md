Are you wondering what damages phone battery and causes it to drain quickly? If so, you're not alone.

Battery problems are a common frustration for many people and there are a number of reasons why your phone battery might be dying so fast.

In this article, I'll tell you the top 5 reasons for phone battery damage and I'll give you tips on how to avoid them.

## 1. Overheating

One of the most common causes of phone battery damage is overheating.

When your phone gets too hot, it can affect the performance and lifespan of the battery, as well as other components of the device. But what happens when a phone overheats, and how can you prevent it?

### What happens when a phone overheats?

Phone overheating can have various causes, such as streaming video content, gaming, high screen brightness, outdated apps or software, exposure to hot conditions or direct sunlight, viruses or malware, damaged battery or charger cable, faulty software, or charging the phone while it is still turned on.

The heat can come from different parts of the phone, such as the battery, the processor, or the screen.

Phone overheating can signal a deeper problem and it is best to consult with the phone's manufacturer or a qualified technician.

Some of the consequences of phone overheating are:

- Reduced battery capacity and efficiency
- Increased risk of battery swelling or explosion
- Slower performance and lagging
- Screen damage or discoloration
- Data loss or corruption
- Hardware failure or malfunction

### How to prevent your phone from overheating

The good news is that there are some simple steps you can take to keep your phone cool and avoid overheating.

Here are some tips:

- Avoid using your phone while it is charging
- Use a certified charger and cable that are compatible with your device
- Turn off unnecessary features such as Bluetooth, GPS, Wi-Fi, or mobile data when not in use
- Lower the screen brightness and timeout settings
- Close background apps that are not in use or consuming too much power
- Update your apps and software regularly to fix any bugs or glitches- Delete any apps that you don't use or that cause overheating
- Scan your phone for viruses or malware using antivirus software.
- Avoid exposing your phone to extreme temperatures or direct sunlight
- Use a cooling pad or fan to dissipate the heat from your phone
- Remove any case or cover that may trap heat inside your phone

By following these tips, you can prevent your phone from overheating and extend its battery life.

## 2. Using apps that drain the battery

Another reason why your phone battery may get damaged is that you are using apps that drain the battery excessively.

Some apps consume more power than others, especially if they run in the background, use location services, stream video or audio, or send frequent notifications.

These apps can cause your phone to overheat and reduce your battery life.

### What kind of apps drain the battery?

The following apps are known to drain battery:

- **Social media apps** such as Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, Snapchat, YouTube, and WhatsApp. These apps use multiple features such as camera, microphone, GPS, Wi-Fi, mobile data, and push notifications. They also tend to keep you hooked on your screen for long periods of time.
- **Dating apps** such as Bumble, Grindr, and Tinder. These apps also use location services, notifications, and mobile data to match you with potential partners. They can also be addictive and time-consuming.
- **Google apps** such as Google Maps, Chrome, Gmail, Google Pay, Google Photos, Google Assistant, and Google Home. These apps require the use of GPS, internet connection, and sync services to provide you with information and convenience. Especially those that require the use of GPS, like Google Maps.
- **Streaming apps** such as Netflix, Spotify, TikTok, and Twitch. These apps use a lot of bandwidth and processing power to deliver high-quality video and audio content. They also keep your screen on for long durations.
- **Other apps** such as Uber, Amazon Alexa, Waze, Fitbit, Skype, and Verizon. These apps use various features such as location services, voice recognition, Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, mobile data, and notifications to offer you different services and functions.

### How to reduce battery drain from apps

There are some simple steps you can take to reduce battery drain from apps and extend your phone's battery life. Here are some tips:

- Check your battery usage statistics in your phone's settings to see which apps are consuming the most power. You can also use third-party apps such as [AccuBattery](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.digibites.accubattery) to monitor your battery health and performance.
- Uninstall or disable any apps that you don't use or need. You can also force stop or clear cache for any apps that are running in the background unnecessarily.
- Adjust your app settings to limit their access to certain features such as location services, notifications, sync services, or background data. You can also turn on battery saver mode or dark mode for some apps to reduce their power consumption.
- Update your apps regularly to fix any bugs or glitches that may cause battery drain. You can also check for app reviews or ratings to see if other users have reported any issues with battery drain.
- Use Wi-Fi instead of mobile data whenever possible to save battery. You can also turn off Wi-Fi when not in use or switch to airplane mode when you don't need any connectivity.
- Avoid using your phone while it is charging or use a certified charger and cable that are compatible with your device. You can also avoid fast charging or wireless charging if they cause your phone to overheat.

By following these tips, you can reduce battery drain from apps and extend your phone's battery life.

## 3. Physical damage

Another factor that can damage your phone battery is physical damage. This can happen due to accidents, mishandling, or improper care of your device.

Physical damage can affect the battery itself or the phone as a whole, leading to reduced performance, overheating, or even fire hazards.

### What can cause physical damage to a phone battery?

There are several ways to damage your phone battery physically:

- **Using cheap or damaged cables with your phone**: These can cause short circuits, power surges, or overheating that can harm your battery or your phone's charging port.
- **Subjecting the phone to extreme heat:** This can cause the battery to swell, leak, or explode. Heat can also damage other parts of the phone, such as the screen, the processor, or the camera.
- **Dropping your phone on a hard surface:** This can cause physical damage to the battery or the phone's casing, affecting its functionality and safety .

### How to prevent physical damage to your phone battery

There are simple tips you should know to prevent physical damage to your phone battery and extend its lifespan.

Here are some tips:

- Use a protective case or cover for your phone that can absorb shocks and prevent scratches. Also, avoid putting your phone in your pocket with keys, coins, or other sharp objects that may damage it.
- Handle your phone with care and avoid dropping it or throwing it around. If you notice any signs of physical damage, such as cracks, dents, bulges, or leaks, take your phone to a professional repair service as soon as possible.
- Avoid exposing your phone to extreme temperatures or direct sunlight. Keep your phone in a cool and dry place and avoid leaving it in a car, near a heater, or under direct sunlight for long periods.
- Use a certified charger and cable that are compatible with your device. Avoid using cheap or damaged accessories that may not meet the safety standards of your phone.

By following these tips, you can prevent physical damage to your phone battery and extend its lifespan.

## 4. Keeping your phone's screen brightness too high

One other thing that can damage your phone battery is keeping your phone's screen brightness too high.

This can happen due to using your phone in bright environments, having the adaptive brightness feature on, or simply preferring a brighter display.

Keeping your phone's screen brightness too high can affect your battery's performance, longevity, and safety.

### Why does keeping your phone's screen brightness too high damage your battery?

The screen is one of the most power-hungry components of your phone. The brighter the screen, the more power it consumes.

This means that keeping your phone's screen brightness too high will drain your battery faster and reduce its lifespan.

It will also generate more heat, which can damage your battery and other parts of your phone.

### How to keep your phone's screen brightness at a lower setting

There are some simple steps you can take to keep your phone's screen brightness at a lower setting and save your battery life. Here are some tips:

- Adjust your screen brightness manually according to the ambient light. You can do this by swiping down from the top of the screen and using the brightness slider. Try to keep it as low as possible while still being comfortable for your eyes.
- Turn off the adaptive brightness feature if you have it on. This feature automatically adjusts the screen brightness based on the amount of light detected. While this feature is designed to help save battery power, it can also make your screen too bright in some situations. You can turn it off by going to Settings > Display > Adaptive Brightness and toggling it off.
- Use dark mode or night mode for some apps or websites that support it. This will reduce the amount of light emitted by the screen and save battery power. You can enable dark mode or night mode by going to Settings > Display > Dark Mode or Night Mode and toggling it on. You can also use third-party apps or extensions that can force dark mode or night mode on any app or website.
- Reduce the screen timeout or use smart lock features to turn off the screen when not in use. The longer the screen stays on, the more power it consumes. You can reduce the screen timeout by going to Settings > Display > Screen Timeout and choosing a shorter duration. You can also use smart lock features such as face unlock, fingerprint unlock, or trusted devices to unlock your phone without turning on the screen.

By following these tips, you can keep your phone's screen brightness at a lower setting and protect your battery from damage.

## 5. Not following the best charging practices

Another factor that can damage your phone battery is not following the best charging practices.

This can happen due to using the wrong charger or cable, charging your phone too often or for too long, or charging your phone when it is too hot or too cold.

Not following the best charging practices can affect your battery's performance, longevity, and safety.

### What are the best charging practices for phone batteries?

To increase the lifespan of your cell phone battery, you should follow these best charging practices:

- Store and charge your phone at moderate temperatures. Avoid exposing your phone to extreme heat or cold, as this can cause the battery to swell, leak, or explode. Heat can also damage other parts of the phone, such as the screen, the processor, or the camera.
- Turn off your device or allow it to idle while charging. Avoid using your phone while it is charging, especially for intensive tasks such as gaming, streaming, or browsing. This can cause your phone to overheat and reduce your battery life.
- Use a certified charger and cable that are compatible with your device. Avoid using cheap or damaged accessories that may not meet the safety standards of your phone. These can cause short circuits, power surges, or overheating that can harm your battery or your phone's charging port.

## Frequently Asked Questions on what damages phone battery

Here are some Frequently Asked Questions on what damages phone battery.

### Does low current damage phone battery?

No, low current does not damage phone battery, but it may affect its charging speed and efficiency.

Use a suitable charger and cable that can deliver enough current for your battery.

### Does power bank kill phone battery?

No, using a good and compatible power bank does not kill phone battery.

However, using a bad or incompatible power bank can damage your phone battery and phone.

### Does bad battery affect phone performance?

Yes, bad battery can affect phone performance.

A bad battery is a battery that has lost its capacity and efficiency over time. It can make your phone slower, hotter, or less reliable.

To prevent your battery from becoming bad, you should charge it properly, avoid extreme temperatures, use a good charger and cable, and replace it when needed.

### Is using your phone while charging bad?

Yes, using your phone while charging is bad. It can overheat, slow charge, or damage your phone and battery.

Use a good charger and cable, and avoid using your phone while charging.

Unplug your phone when it is full.

Stop using your phone if it gets too hot or the cable breaks.

### Is it bad to charge your phone multiple times a day?

No, it is not bad to charge your phone multiple times a day, if you do it right.

Charge your phone between 20-80%, use a good charger and cable, avoid using your phone while charging, and keep your phone in a cool place. This will help your battery last longer.
